HEALTHCARE DATASET — FINAL SUMMARY REPORT
Overview
This project analyzes a healthcare dataset to understand how demographics, lifestyle habits, and medical conditions influence patient health outcomes and hospital visits.
The analysis focuses on key variables such as:
Age
Gender
BMI
Blood Pressure
Diabetes
Smoking Status
Exercise Frequency
Hospital Visits
Key Insights:
 1. Age & Hospital Visits
 Seniors tend to have higher average hospital visits
Younger groups visit less frequently

2. Smoking vs Diabetes
Smokers show a higher proportion of diabetes cases compared to non-smokers

 3. Exercise vs Diabetes
Patients who exercise frequently have lower diabetes.

 4. BMI Distribution
Majority of patients fall within Normal and Overweight ranges

 5. Blood Pressure by Age Group
Hypertension increases with age

 6. Gender Distribution
Dataset shows a relatively balanced distribution between.
 Dashboard Features
Interactive slicers (Gender, Diabetes, Smoking, Exercise)
KPI cards (Total Patients, Avg BMI, Avg Visits, % Diabetic)
Combination of:
Clustered charts
100% stacked charts
Line/Combo charts
Distribution visuals
Conclusion
The analysis reveals that:
Lifestyle factors (smoking, exercise) significantly impact health outcomes
Age and BMI are major drivers of hospital visits and health risks
Preventive measures such as regular exercise and weight management can reduce disease prevalence
